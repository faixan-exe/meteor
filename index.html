What is Origami?

Origami designs are intricate and artistic creations made by skillfully folding a single sheet of paper into various shapes, figures, and formsâ€”without the use of cutting tools, glue, or additional materials. This minimalist approach highlights the beauty of transformation and precision. The word originates from the Japanese terms ori (meaning "to fold") and kami (meaning "paper"), reflecting its deep cultural roots in Japan. Traditionally, origami was used for ceremonial purposes and as a form of storytelling, with iconic designs like the paper crane symbolizing peace and good fortune.
